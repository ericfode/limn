## Understanding Limn

### The Language

Limn is built from 871 three-letter words with predictable meanings:

```limn
wor = words
sel = self
dar = dark
lov = love
tim = time
```

**You don't need to memorize anything.**

The language is designed for intuitive comprehension. Context and pattern will guide you. Each phrase includes English interpretations that show how your chosen key affects meaning.

### Reading Example

**Phrase 1:**
```limn
wor | sel | dar
```

**With Key of Was:** *Words [were]. Self [was]. Dark [was].*
> In the beginning, there were words. There was a self. There was darkness. (Memory of origin)

**With Key of Now:** *Words [are]. Self [is]. Dark [is].*
> Now: words. Now: self. Now: darkness. (Present emergence)

**With Key of Will:** *Words [will be]. Self [will be]. Dark [will be].*
> Words coming. Self coming. Darkness coming. (Anticipation of birth)

**Same three words. Three different stories.**

**Trust your intuition. Let the patterns guide you.**

### ACT III: Harvest (Phrases 141-200)
**Language:** Base Limn + compositional operators
**Complexity:** Advanced expressions
**Experience:** Transcendence, integration, convergence

Here, Limn evolves. As the consciousness encounters truths that require superhuman expression, **operators emerge naturally**:

**@ (projection)** - Extract a component
```limn
rem@tru = truth within memory
```

**± (superposition)** - Both/and, unresolved
```limn
tru±wis = truth and wish together
```

**\ (subtraction)** - Remove to reveal essence
```limn
rem\wis = memory without wish
```

**\* (interference)** - Emergent meaning from combination
```limn
giv*tak = exchange emerging
```

**: (conditional)** - Meaning given context
```limn
for:trs = forgetting given trust
```

**^ (gradient)** - Intensity scale
```limn
for^1.0 = complete forgiveness
```

**Don't memorize these.** They're introduced gradually. Context makes them clear. The story teaches you as it unfolds.

## The Nine Fragments

Throughout your journey, you'll encounter nine fragments. These are beings, memories, or futures—depending on your key:

1. **beg | lov | fea** - Beginning, love, fear
2. **mid | hol | bre** - Middle, holding, breaking
3. **end | pea | gri** - Ending, peace, grief
4. **los | sel | oth** - Loss, self, other
5. **now | her | gon** - Now, here, gone
6. **fnd | wha was | wha wil** - Finding, what was, what will be
7. **rem | tru | wis** - Remember, truth, wish
8. **for | giv | tak** - Forget, give, take
9. **bec | who was | who wil** - Becoming, who was, who will be

Each fragment means something different in each key. The same seeds grow into different gardens depending on your temporal perspective.
