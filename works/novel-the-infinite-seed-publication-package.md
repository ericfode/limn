## Discussion Questions (For Reading Groups)

1. **Which temporal key did you choose? Why? How do you think your choice reflects your own relationship with time?**

2. **How did knowing that other readers experienced different stories affect your reading? Did it make you want to reread with a different key?**

3. **In Act III, compositional operators emerge. Did they enhance or obscure meaning for you? Can you think of emotions or states that have no simple English equivalent?**

4. **Which of the nine fragments resonated most strongly? How did your temporal key shape your connection to them?**

5. **Phrase 177 expresses integration through composition: `you = (beg±mid±end) * (los±now±fnd) * (rem±for±bec)`. How would you express this in English? What's gained or lost in translation?**

6. **The novel argues that temporal perspective doesn't just frame experience—it creates meaning. Do you agree? How does this relate to memory, anticipation, and presence in your own life?**

7. **Would this story work in English? What's essential about it being told in Limn?**

8. **The final phrase (200) converges all three keys on the same truth. Did this resolution satisfy you? What is the shared truth?**

9. **If you've read with multiple keys: How did the experience differ? Did it feel like reading different novels or different interpretations of the same novel?**

10. **THE INFINITE SEED explores consciousness awakening/fragmenting/transforming. Which interpretation (death, birth, or transformation) feels truest to you? Why?**
