# Limn Conversations

*Example dialogues demonstrating Limn in practice*

---

Conversation in Limn is unlike natural language conversation. Without word order or explicit subjects, each utterance describes a region of meaning that both parties navigate together. The **shared key** develops through the exchange itself.

These examples show how meaning emerges, refines, and occasionally breaks down.

---

## Conversation 1: Two Scientists

**Context:** Two marine biologists discussing observations from a dive.

---

**Dr. A:** `lif gro bel aqu | col nox`

*Constraints: alive + growth + below + water | cold + dark*

**Dr. B's interpretation:** "Life growing in the deep cold darkness of the ocean. You saw something in the abyssal zone?"

---

**Dr. B:** `wh? lif gro?`

*Constraints: what? + alive + growth?*

**Dr. A's interpretation:** "What kind of growing life?"

---

**Dr. A:** `mic flo man | spi lon bri`

*Constraints: microscopic + flowing + many | spiral + long + bright*

**Dr. B's interpretation:** "Microscopic things flowing in great numbers, with long bright spirals. Bioluminescent plankton with flagella? Or tube worms near a vent?"

---

**Dr. B:** `the loc? ori pyr?`

*Constraints: the + location? + origin + fire/heat?*

**Dr. A's interpretation:** "Near a hydrothermal vent?" (Interpreting `pyr` as heat source)

---

**Dr. A:** `yes | ori pyr | lif gro ext`

*Constraints: yes | origin + heat | alive + growth + extreme*

**Dr. B's interpretation:** "Yes, near a vent. Extremophile life."

---

**Dr. B:** `new? kno? nu kno?`

*Constraints: new? + known? + not-known?*

**Dr. A's interpretation:** "Is it a new species, known, or unknown?"

---

**Dr. A:** `nu kno | nu sim | uni pos`

*Constraints: not-known | not-similar | unique + possible*

**Dr. B's interpretation:** "Unknown. Not similar to anything documented. Possibly unique."

---

**Dr. B:** `imp | pub beg | nam?`

*Constraints: important | publish + beginning | name?*

**Dr. A's interpretation:** "This is important. We should start publishing. What shall we name it?"

---

### Notes on Conversation 1

- The key develops through context: marine biology, deep ocean, vent ecosystems
- Questions use `wh?` for open queries, `?` suffix for yes/no
- Confirmation (`yes`) is explicit when needed
- Scientific terminology emerges from constraint intersection: `mic flo bri` = bioluminescent microorganisms
- The conversation moves from observation to classification to action

## Conversation 2: Two Friends After Absence

**Context:** Two people who haven't spoken in years, meeting by chance.

---

**Friend A:** `yo! you old fri | dur lon awa`

*Constraints: this/here! + young + old + friend | duration + long + away*

**Friend B's interpretation:** "Hey! Old friend who I've known since we were young. It's been so long since we've been together."

---

**Friend B:** `yes | dur tra sel | sel nu sam`

*Constraints: yes | duration + transformation + self | self + not + same*

**Friend A's interpretation:** "Yes. Over time, I've transformed. I'm not the same person."

---

**Friend A:** `sel tra sel | nu bad | gro`

*Constraints: self + transformation + self | not + bad | growth*

**Friend B's interpretation:** "You've transformed into yourself. That's not bad - that's growth."

---

**Friend B:** `tha | yo | fee mem | pas joy sad`

*Constraints: thanks | this/here | feeling + memory | past + joy + sadness*

**Friend A's interpretation:** "Thank you. Right now I'm feeling memories - the past holds both joy and sadness."

---

**Friend A:** `pas | jo res | sad mov`

*Constraints: past | joy + resting | sadness + moving*

**Friend B's interpretation:** "The past: the joy has settled; the sadness is still moving through."

---

**Friend B:** `for? | joi ret?`

*Constraints: forward? | joining + return?*

**Friend A's interpretation:** "Looking forward? Should we reconnect?"

---

**Friend A:** `for joi | nu ret pas | cre new`

*Constraints: forward + join | not + return + past | create + new*

**Friend B's interpretation:** "Let's connect going forward. We won't return to the past - we'll create something new."

---

### Notes on Conversation 2

- The key is personal relationship history
- Emotional states expressed through constraint intersection: `fee mem | pas joy sad`
- Time handled through `pas` (past), `dur` (duration), `for` (forward)
- The `|` operator separates distinct emotional states
- Resolution comes through shared understanding of what "forward" means

## Conversation 3: Negotiation

**Context:** Two parties negotiating a business deal with some tension.

---

**Party A:** `yo wan | giv X | tak Y`

*Constraints: I + want | give X | take Y*

**Party B's interpretation:** "I want to give X in exchange for taking Y." (X and Y are pre-established referents)

---

**Party B:** `Y mag | X min | nu equ`

*Constraints: Y + large | X + small | not + equal*

**Party A's interpretation:** "Y is too large, X is too small. This isn't an equal exchange."

---

**Party A:** `X mor? | Y les?`

*Constraints: X + more? | Y + less?*

**Party B's interpretation:** "Should I give more X? Should you give less Y?"

---

**Party B:** `X mor and Y les | pos equ`

*Constraints: X + more + and + Y + less | possible + equal*

**Party A's interpretation:** "More X AND less Y would make it possibly equal."

---

**Party A:** `X mor | nec | Y les | nu pos`

*Constraints: X + more | necessary | Y + less | not + possible*

**Party B's interpretation:** "I can give more X if necessary. But you giving less Y isn't possible for me to accept."

---

**Party B:** `blo | Y nec | sel exi dut`

*Constraints: blocking | Y + necessary | self + exist + duty*

**Party A's interpretation:** "That's a blocker. Y is necessary - it's existential for us, a duty."

---

**Party A:** `und | new pat?`

*Constraints: understand | new + path?*

**Party B's interpretation:** "I understand. Is there a new path forward?"

---

**Party B:** `Y div | par yo | par an | equ pos`

*Constraints: Y + divide | part + this/here | part + that/there | equal + possible*

**Party A's interpretation:** "What if we divide Y? Part here (with you), part there (with us). That could be equal."

---

**Party A:** `cre | agr | for joi`

*Constraints: creative | agree | forward + join*

**Party B's interpretation:** "That's creative. I agree. Let's move forward together."

---

### Notes on Conversation 3

- Business key allows shorthand for pre-established referents (X, Y)
- Quantitative concepts expressed: `mag` (large), `min` (small), `mor` (more), `les` (less)
- Logical structure: conditions, possibilities, necessities
- The `|` operator separates proposals and counters
- Resolution through creative reframing: `Y div | par yo | par an`

## Conversation 4: Parent and Child

**Context:** A parent explaining something to a young child.

---

**Parent:** `yo ani | fis | lif ins aqu`

*Constraints: this + animal | fish | alive + inside + water*

**Child's understanding:** "This animal is a fish. It lives inside water."

---

**Child:** `wh lif ins aqu?`

*Constraints: why + alive + inside + water?*

**Parent's interpretation:** "Why does it live inside water?"

---

**Parent:** `fis cor | nec aqu | aer nu pos`

*Constraints: fish + core/body | need + water | air + not + possible*

**Child's understanding:** "The fish's body needs water. Air isn't possible for it."

---

**Child:** `yo | aer nec | aqu nu pos?`

*Constraints: I/this | air + need | water + not + possible?*

**Parent's interpretation:** "Do I need air? Is water not possible for me?"

---

**Parent:** `yes | yo hum | aqu ins lon | nu pos`

*Constraints: yes | you + human | water + inside + long | not + possible*

**Child's understanding:** "Yes. You're human. Being inside water for a long time isn't possible."

---

**Child:** `fis sad? | nu aer`

*Constraints: fish + sad? | no + air*

**Parent's interpretation:** "Is the fish sad? Because it can't have air?"

---

**Parent:** `nu | fis joy aqu | yo joy aer | dif | goo`

*Constraints: no | fish + joy + water | you + joy + air | different | good*

**Child's understanding:** "No. Fish are happy in water. You're happy in air. Different, but that's good."

---

### Notes on Conversation 4

- Key is pedagogical: simple explanations
- `wh` for "why" questions
- Parent uses direct constraints; child asks for clarification
- Emotional concepts like `sad` and `joy` enter naturally
- The lesson ends with normalization of difference: `dif | goo`

## Conversation 5: Miscommunication

**Context:** A conversation where the key isn't shared and meaning diverges.

---

**Speaker A:** `hot ris ang | nec say`

*A's intended meaning (key: expressing frustration):* "My anger is rising. I need to say something."

**Speaker B's interpretation (key: cooking):* "The heat is rising? You need to say something about... the recipe?"

---

**Speaker B:** `wh hot? tem?`

*B's intended meaning:* "What heat? Temperature?"

**Speaker A's interpretation:** "What anger? Are you dismissing me as temporary?"

---

**Speaker A:** `nu tem | fee dur | yo nu und`

*A's intended meaning:* "This isn't temporary. My feelings are ongoing. You don't understand."

**Speaker B's interpretation:** "The temperature isn't temporary? Your... feeling of duration? I don't understand indeed."

---

**Speaker B:** `cof | key wh?`

*B's meaning:* "I'm confused. What's the key here?"

---

**Speaker A:** `oh | key fee | sel ang | nu key cook`

*A's meaning:* "Oh! The key is feelings. I'm angry. The key isn't cooking."

---

**Speaker B:** `und now | apo | lis | say mor`

*B's meaning:* "I understand now. I apologize. I'm listening. Say more."

---

**Speaker A:** `tha | sel fee ign | wan hea | wan und`

*A's meaning:* "Thank you. I feel ignored. I want to be heard. I want to be understood."

---

**Speaker B:** `sel hea | sel und try | nu per | dur lea`

*B's meaning:* "I hear you. I'm trying to understand. Not perfectly - but ongoing learning."

---

### Notes on Conversation 5

- Demonstrates key mismatch and recovery
- Without shared key, `hot ris` was interpreted literally (heat) vs. metaphorically (anger)
- The explicit key clarification: `key wh?` and `key fee`
- Recovery requires metacommunication about the key itself
- Emotional repair happens through Limn: `apo | lis | say mor`

## Patterns in Limn Conversation

### Key Establishment

Conversations often begin with key-setting, either explicit or through context accumulation.

### Question Markers

- `wh` + constraint = "what is..."
- Constraint + `?` = "is this true?"
- `wh` alone = "what?"

### Turn Structure

Each turn typically offers:
1. Response to previous (optional)
2. New constraint set
3. Sometimes a question for the next turn

### Repair Mechanisms

- `cof` = "I'm confused"
- `key wh?` = "What's the key?"
- `nu und` = "I don't understand"
- Explicit restatement with key

### Affirmation

- `yes` = confirmation
- `agr` = agreement
- `und` = understanding

### Emotional Marking

- `tha` = gratitude
- `apo` = apology
- `fee` + emotion = "I feel..."

## Try Your Own Conversation

1. Establish a shared context (key)
2. Begin with a constraint set describing something
3. Respond with questions, agreements, or elaborations
4. Note where ambiguity helps (poetic) and where it hinders (practical)
5. Practice key repair when misunderstanding occurs
