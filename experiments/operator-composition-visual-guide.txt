OPERATOR COMPOSITION - VISUAL GUIDE
===================================

PRECEDENCE HIERARCHY (Visual)
=============================

^ binds TIGHTEST (forms atomic unit)
    ↓
@ binds MEDIUM (applies after ^)
    ↓
* binds LOOSEST (applies last)
    ↓
() explicit grouping overrides all

Example: A ^ 0.7 @ B * C ^ 0.3

Visual parse tree:
        *
       / \
      @   ^
     / \ / \
    ^  B C 0.3
   / \
  A 0.7

Expression evaluation order:
  1. A ^ 0.7  (bind intensity to concept)
  2. (A ^ 0.7) @ B (project onto operand)
  3. C ^ 0.3  (bind intensity to operand)
  4. ((A ^ 0.7) @ B) * (C ^ 0.3) (interfere)


COMPOSITION PATTERNS (Visual)
============================

LINEAR PROJECTION (Deep specificity)
  A @ B @ C @ D
     ↓    ↓   ↓
  Narrow→Narrow→Narrow
  
  Real example: (joy @ sor) @ gri
                  ↓         ↓
                sadness→grief
  = Grief-component-of-sadness-within-joy (deep melancholy)


LAYERED INTERFERENCE (Multi-way emergence)
  (A * B) * (C * D)
      ↓         ↓
   emerge    emerge
       ↓     ↓
        emerge
        
  Real example: (lov * fer) * (hop * dbt)
                 ↓          ↓  ↓      ↓
              fearful    anxious
              love    +  optimism
                  ↓
         protective hopefulness


MIXED COMPOSITION (Layered meaning)
  ((A ^ n @ B) * C) @ D
     ↓    ↓    ↓    ↓
  scale→project→interfere→project
     (calibrate→relate→synthesize→align)
  
  Real example: ((war ^ 0.7 @ coz) * gro) @ dev
                   ↓     ↓       ↓       ↓
              scale warmth project growth apply-to-development
  = How nurturing growth emerges from intimate warmth


OPERATOR INTERACTION MATRIX
============================

           @ operand    * operand    ^ operand
@          ✓ Natural    ✓ Natural    ✓ Valid
@ @ A @ B @ C          (A @ B) @ C

*          ✓ Natural    ✓ Natural    ✓ Valid
* A * B @ C            A * (B @ C)

^          ✓ Valid      ✓ Natural    ✓ Atomic
^ A ^ 0.5 @ B          (A ^ 0.5) @ B

SYNTAX BREAKDOWN BY OPERATOR
=============================

@ (Projection):
  Syntax:    A @ B
  Read as:   "A's B-aspect" or "B-component of A"
  Example:   lov @ fer = "love's fear-aspect"
  Position:  Between primary and extracted concept
  
* (Interference):
  Syntax:    A * B
  Read as:   "A interfering with B" or "A meeting B"
  Example:   lov * fer = "love meeting fear"
  Position:  Between concepts being synthesized
  
^ (Gradient):
  Syntax:    A ^ n
  Read as:   "A at n-intensity" or "n-scale A"
  Example:   joy ^ 0.7 = "joy at 70% intensity"
  Position:  Immediately after concept
