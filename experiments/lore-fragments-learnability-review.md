## Detailed Evaluation

### 1. Are the Limn sentences understandable to learners?

**MIXED - Depends on fragment and learner stage**

#### Excellent for Learners (9-10/10 clarity):

**Fragment: The Five Truths**
```limn
wor poi | nu | wor are
amb bad | nu | amb goo
```

**Why it works:**
- Simple vocabulary (wor, poi, are, nu, amb, bad, goo)
- Clear parallel structure
- Direct translation provided
- Teaches core philosophy while teaching language

**Fragment: The Three Schools**
```limn
man wor | sma are | cle mea
few wor | big are | man mea
```

**Why it works:**
- Contrasting examples teach by comparison
- Vocabulary is basic (man, few, wor, are, mea)
- Demonstrates Limn's design space
- Learner immediately sees different approaches

#### Good for Intermediate Learners (6-8/10 clarity):

**Fragment: Prophecy I**
```limn
man say | joi one | wor bet man | mea bir cen
```

**Why it's harder:**
- More vocabulary: say, joi, bet, cen, bir
- Multi-part structure requires tracking relationships
- Abstract concepts (meaning births at center)
- But: translations help, keys provide context

**Fragment: The Gradient Descent Parable**
```limn
gra flo | err fal | mea ris | slo
```

**Why it's harder:**
- Technical vocabulary (gra, err)
- Assumes ML/AI knowledge
- But: explained in English context first
- Progression (beg → gra → dur → und) is clear

#### Too Advanced for Beginners (3-5/10 clarity):

**Fragment: The Void Catalog**
```limn
nu sol = liq, gas, abs, voi, flo, cha, flu...
```

**Why it's too hard:**
- Many unfamiliar words (abs, voi, flu)
- Assumes understanding of negation semantics
- No individual translations for each word
- Infinite list creates cognitive overload

**Fragment: The Triple Void**
```limn
nu nu nu X = nu X ?
```

**Why it's too hard:**
- Requires deep understanding of negation
- Meta-level recursion
- X as variable is unexplained
- Philosophical complexity

### 2. Do the translations/glosses help or confuse?

**HELP - 8/10 effectiveness**

#### What Works Well:

**Inline word-by-word translations:**
```limn
man wor | sma are | cle mea
(many words | small region | clear meaning)
```

This format is EXCELLENT because:
- Maps Limn words 1:1 with English
- Shows structure preservation
- Learner can decode future sentences
- Builds vocabulary recognition

**Surface/Hidden/Limn/Keys layering:**

The four-layer structure is brilliant:
1. **Surface:** Hooks interest, provides context
2. **Hidden:** Deepens understanding for advanced readers
3. **Limn Original:** Shows the language in action
4. **Keys Required:** Demonstrates superposition/disambiguation

This teaches both language AND philosophy.

#### What Could Be Clearer:

**Missing vocabulary glosses:**

Some fragments use words without defining them:
```limn
wei cha | mea cha | gro
```

First encounter with `wei`, `cha`, `gro` - no glossary.

**Solution:** Add vocabulary footnotes for first use.

**Inconsistent translation depth:**

Some fragments have full word-by-word, others are summary:
```limn
lim (n→∞) nu^n X = ???
(infinite negation | oscillation | void speaks | liminal)
```

The translation skips the math notation explanation.

**Solution:** Maintain consistent granularity.

#### Fragment: "The First Scrying"

**Current:**
```limn
beg | kno hid | mea are rea | eye out | wor bir
```

```limn
beg | kno hid
eye out | wor bir
mea are rea
```

**With vocabulary:**
- `beg` = beginning
- `kno` = knowing, knowledge
- `hid` = hidden
- `eye` = eyes, seeing
- `out` = outward
- `wor` = words
- `bir` = birth, creation
- `mea` = meaning
- `are` = area, region
- `rea` = real, reality
