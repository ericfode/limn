**Two natural strata have emerged:**

| Stratum | Pattern | Count | Examples | Character |
|---------|---------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Pronounceable** | CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant) | ~550 | `lov`, `sol`, `hot`, `fer` | Human-friendly, intuitive |
| **Compact** | Consonant clusters (CCC, VCC, CCV) | ~1,450 | `str`, `prl`, `crn`, `drft` | Technical, dense |

**Why two strata work:**
- **Pronounceable stratum** anchors human comprehension — these are the words you teach first, the ones that "sound like" their meaning

**The fixed-width constraint is the real design principle.** Three characters means:
- Consistent information density (3.65 bits/char, 77.7% of theoretical max)
- Clean tokenization and parsing (no delimiters needed between operators)
- Compositional operators bind unambiguously to fixed-width operands
- 17,576-slot namespace provides growth runway for decades

**Namespace economics:** Composition, not vocabulary growth, is the scaling mechanism. The 6 operators multiply ~2,000 base words into 30,000+ expressions. The tri-letter namespace will not saturate before composition makes base-word expansion unnecessary.

## Part III: Compositional Operators (The Revolution)

### Overview: 6 LLM-Native Operators

Limn v4 introduces operators that map to operations LLMs perform naturally in embedding space:

| Operator | Name | Function | Example |
|----------|------|----------|---------|
| `@` | Projection | Extract B-component of A | `lov@fer` = fear in love |
| `*` | Interference | Emergent meaning from A and B | `sol*liq` = gel |
| `^` | Gradient | Intensity parameter (0-1) | `big^0.7` = fairly big |
| `without` | Subtraction | A with B removed | `king without man` = royalty |
| `±` | Superposition | Quantum both/and | `yes±no` = undecided |
| `given` | Conditional | A given B context | `lov given trs` = love with trust |

**Precedence:** `^` > `@` > `*` > `without` > `given` > `±`

**Symbol aliases:** `\` for `without`, `:` for `given` — accepted in dense expressions but word forms are canonical for teaching and comprehension. Symbol aliases exist for backward compatibility and expert shorthand.

---

### Operator 1: Projection (@)

**Semantic:** Extract the B-component of A (vector projection in embedding space)

**Intuition:** "What aspect of A aligns with B?"

**Examples:**
```
lov@fer = fear-component of love (anxiety of loss)
joy@sad = sadness within joy (bittersweet)
cur@fer = courage's relationship to fear (facing fear)
ang@fer = fear beneath anger (defensive reaction)
```

**Properties:**
- Non-commutative: `A@B ≠ B@A`
- Chains work: `(A@B)@C` extracts C-component of A's B-component
- Reflexive intensifies: `A@A` = pure/essential A

**Test yourself:**
- What does `hop@dbt` mean? (doubt-component of hope = uncertainty in hoping)
- What does `trs@los` mean? (loss-component of trust = vulnerability of trusting)

---

### Operator 2: Interference (*)

**Semantic:** Create emergent meaning from interference of A and B

**Intuition:** "What state is both A and B simultaneously?"

**Examples:**
```
sol*liq = gel/glass (neither solid nor liquid)
joy*sad = bittersweet (both emotions interfering)
lov*fer = passion/obsession (love and fear together)
hot*col = thermal shock/lukewarm (temperature interference)
```

**Properties:**
- Commutative: `A*B = B*A`
- Multi-way: `A*B*C` creates triple interference
- Intensification: `A**B` = stronger interference

**Test yourself:**
- What does `new*old` mean? (liminal between new and old)
- What does `win*los` mean? (stalemate, pyrrhic victory)

---

### Operator 3: Gradient (^)

**Semantic:** Intensity parameter from 0.0 (none) to 1.0 (maximum)

**Intuition:** "How much of A?"

**Examples:**
```
big^0.1 = barely big (tiny)
big^0.5 = moderately big (medium)
big^0.7 = considerably big (large)
big^1.0 = maximally big (huge)

lov^0.3 = slight love (affection)
lov^0.9 = intense love (devotion)

hot^0.2 = slightly hot (warm)
hot^0.8 = very hot (scalding)
```

**Properties:**
- Continuous scale (not discrete like +/-)
- Combines with other operators: `(A^0.7)@B`, `A^0.5*B^0.5`
- Precise control over intensity

**Test yourself:**
- What does `sad^0.4` mean? (moderately sad, melancholy)
- What does `tru^0.9` mean? (very high trust, nearly absolute)

---

### Operator 4: Subtraction (without)

**Semantic:** A with B-component removed (semantic algebra)

**Intuition:** "What is A without its B aspect?"

**Syntax:** `A without B` (alias: `A\B`)

**Examples:**
```
king without man = royalty essence (kingship without maleness)
tea without wat = tea concentrate (tea without water)
lov without fer = secure love (love without fear component)
joy without sad = pure joy (joy without any sadness)
```

**Properties:**
- Reveals essence by removal
- Classic semantic algebra: `king without man + wom = queen`
- Isolates core properties
- Word form reads naturally: "A without B" is self-explanatory

**Test yourself:**
- What does `fam without bon` mean? (family members without bonds)
- What does `dar without lig` mean? (darkness without light = void)

---

### Operator 5: Superposition (±)

**Semantic:** Quantum both/and (unresolved superposition)

**Intuition:** "Both A and B exist simultaneously in unresolved state"

**Examples:**
```
yes±no = undecided/ambivalent (both answers active)
tru±fal = paradox (both true and false)
her±the = non-binary (both/neither gender)
lov±hat = ambivalence (love and hate coexisting)
```

**Properties:**
- Captures pre-collapse quantum states
- Different from `*` (interference creates new state)
- `±` maintains both original states unresolved

**Test yourself:**
- What does `kno±unk` mean? (knowing and not-knowing paradox)
- What does `pre±abs` mean? (both present and absent)

---

### Operator 6: Conditional (given)

**Semantic:** A in the context of B (conditional relationship)

**Intuition:** "A, given that B applies"

**Syntax:** `A given B` (alias: `A:B`)

**Examples:**
```
lov given trs = love given trust exists
fer given dan = fear when danger is present
joy given tim = joy experienced over time (nostalgia)
kno given exp = knowledge through experience
```

**Properties:**
- Contextualizes meaning
- Enables temporal/conditional reasoning
- Clarifies scope of application
- Word form is self-documenting: "A given B" reads as natural English

**Test yourself:**
- What does `hop given los` mean? (hope in context of loss)
- What does `cal given sto` mean? (calm given storm)

## Part IV: Compositional Patterns

### Simple Compositions (2 operators)

**Projection chains:**
```
(A@B)@C = C-component of A's B-aspect
Example: (lov@fer)@anx = anxiety within the fear in love
```

**Gradient projections:**
```
(A^n)@B = Extract B-aspect of A at intensity n
Example: (joy^0.8)@sad = strong sadness-within-joy (deep bittersweet)
```

**Interference with intensity:**
```
A^n*B^m = Weighted interference
Example: lov^0.7*fer^0.3 = mostly love with some fear (anxious affection)
```

### Complex Compositions (3+ operators)

**Triple combinations:**
```
((A@B)*C)^n = Extract component, interfere, then scale
Example: ((lov@fer)*hop)^0.9 = intense hopeful-fearful love
```

**Conditional gradients:**
```
A^n given B = Intensity n of A, given B
Example: joy^0.5 given tim = moderate joy over time (gentle nostalgia)
```

**Nested projections:**
```
A@(B*C) = Project A onto emergent state B*C
Example: thi@(kno*dbt) = thought projected onto uncertain knowledge
```

### Optimal Complexity

**Best practice:** 2-3 operators per expression
- 1 operator: Simple modification
- 2 operators: Sweet spot (expressive + readable)
- 3 operators: Complex but manageable
- 4+ operators: Use sparingly (high cognitive load)
